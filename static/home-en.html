Heinrich Bullinger, Portrait by Hans Asper, 1545

Heinrich Bullinger was the successor of Huldrych Zwingli in Zurich and played a key role in disseminating the ideas of the Reformation in Switzerland and Europe. His correspondence is of great historical significance. The 12,000 letters provide fascinating insights into the political events and theological debates of the time, but also into the economic circumstances and everyday life of people in the early modern period.
