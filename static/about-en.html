What you can find in the online search system

The starting point for Bullinger Digital was the information on the Bullinger correspondence provided by the Institute for Swiss Reformation Studies (IRG), the project partner and publisher of the Heinrich Bullinger Correspondence Edition. Additionally, information from other knowledge sources is available. The following overview shows the information available in the online search system:
  • Metadata for all preserved letters, compiled by the Institute for Swiss Reformation Studies (IRG).
  • Summaries (regests) for all letters published in the Heinrich Bullinger Correspondence Edition (HBBW Edition) (= letters from 1523–1547).
  • Transcriptions of letter texts
    • from the HBBW Edition
    • from other editions, e.g., Vadian Collection of Letters (ed. by Emil Arbenz 1890–1913) or Bullinger’s Correspondence with the Grisons (ed. by Traugott Schiess, 1904–1906)
    • unpublished transcriptions by the HBBW editors for volumes 16-20
    • preliminary transcriptions of yet-to-be-edited letters (= letters from 1548–1575), some with notes, summaries, or uncertainty markings
    • machine-generated transcriptions for which no transcriptions are available, but facsimiles exist
    Machine-generated transcriptions are clearly marked as such; the source of other transcriptions is indicated with .
  • Editorial notes and factual comments
    • from the HBBW Edition
    • by HBBW editors for some unpublished letters in volumes 16-20
    • as preliminary notes by HBBW editors
    The respective source is identified with .
  • Facsimiles of 11,000 letters preserved in Zurich memory institutions and 200 facsimiles from the Basel University Library, Graubünden State Archive, and Zofingen City Library. The origin of the facsimiles is displayed above the digital copies and must be correctly cited when used (see Copyright).
  • Additional facsimiles available as PDFs on www.swisscollections.ch are linked.
