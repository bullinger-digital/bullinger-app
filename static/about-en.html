What will you find in Bullinger Digital?

  • Metadata for all preserved letters, compiled by the Heinrich Bullinger Edition Correspondence (HBBW) team.

  • Abstracts (summaries) for all letters published in the HBBW Edition (= letters from 1523 to April 1548).

  • Transcriptions of the letter texts

    • from the HBBW Edition

    • from other editions, e.g., Vadian Letter Collection (ed. Emil Arbenz, 1890–1913) or Bullinger’s Correspondence with the Grisons (ed. Traugott Schiess, 1904–1906)

    • created but unpublished transcriptions by the HBBW team for volumes 16–20

    • preliminary transcriptions by the HBBW team of unpublished letters (= letters from May 1548 to 1575), partially with annotations, summaries, or uncertainty markers

    • machine-generated transcriptions where no transcription is yet available, but a facsimile (scan image) exists

The source and type of transcription is indicated in the next to the heading “Letter Text.”

No transcriptions are available for letters for which we have no facsimiles.

  • Editorial notes and comments

    • from the HBBW Edition

    • by the HBBW team for some unpublished letter texts in volumes 16–20

    • as preliminary notes by the HBBW team

  • Facsimiles of 11,000 letters preserved in Zurich memory institutions, and 200 facsimiles from the University Library of Basel, the State Archive of Graubünden, the Zofingen City Library, and the Cantonal Archive of Glarus. Proper citation is required when using facsimiles (see Copyright). Additional facsimiles that are available as PDFs on www.swisscollections.ch are linked.

  • Indexes for

    • Persons mentioned in the letters or acting as correspondents. Each person has an information page with:

      • Links to resources or projects (if available)

      • Name variants

      • Interactive list of letters mentioning or involving the person

      • Selected footnotes (from the HBBW Edition) with additional information about the person (if available)

    • Places mentioned or designated as locations of sending or receiving a letter

    • Institutions/groups acting as correspondents

    • Archives where the letters are preserved

    • Literature referenced or cited in the HBBW comments. Often only abbreviations are used; these are spelled-out in the literature index

Which letters have facsimiles?

  • Facsimiles are available for a total of 11,200 letters.

  • We differentiate between:

    • Autograph = document written and sent by the sender

    • Original = not written by the sender but sent by him/her

    • (autograph) Draft = draft letter written by the sender

    • (autograph) Copy / Partial copy = transcript of the letter written by the sender

  • If no facsimile of the autograph or the original is available, facsimiles of copies or drafts are provided whenever possible. These may differ from the transcribed text if they are based on a different document type.

What search functions are available?

  • You can restrict your search to the following fields:

    • Letter text

    • Abstract

    • Footnotes

    • Printed

    • Notes

    • Normalized German (i.e., historical spelling for Early New High German searchable in modern German spelling)

  • You can search for a specific spelling by placing the term in quotation marks. Special characters can be selected via .

  • For language-independent searches, use the automatically generated English translations, available for download here.

  • You can search by the following metadata (filters):

    • Sender

    • Recipient

    • Institution / Group

    • Location

    • Topics (= automatically assigned tags)

    • Timeline (interactive)

  • You can also set additional filters for:

    • Mentioned persons

    • Mentioned locations

    • Letter language

    • Archive (= storage location of the manuscript)

    • Signature (= manuscript identifier)

    • HBBW number (= letter number in printed edition)

    • Letter ID (= file number in online edition)

    • Facsimile availability

If you set different filters, these are AND-linked, i.e. the letters displayed fulfill all criteria. If you make a selection of several values in the same filter field, these are OR-linked, i.e. entries with at least one of these values are displayed.

How can you navigate the letters?

  • When you open a letter, navigation arrows appear above the metadata.

    • Use single arrows to browse previous/next letter

    • Use double arrows to browse previous/next letter between the same correspondents (same letter writers)

  • You can access specific letters via the lists under "Index"

What additional features are available?

  • Language display
    You can color-mark the languages used in the letter text by clicking the “Mark languages” box above the letter text.

  • Name display
    You can highlight person and place names mentioned in a letter (in the summary, text, and footnotes) by clicking “Highlight names” above the metadata. Clicking on an underlined name shows the indexed name. Clicking on the indexed name opens its detail page (see Index). This option is only available for manually transcribed letters.

  • Notes and references
    Unedited letters may have multiple entries in the search system, e.g., due to uncertainty about the date or unresolved combinations of letters and enclosures. References typically lack facsimile and transcription and are linked to the relevant letter.

  • Online version of HBBW volumes
    All edited letters are linked to the respective HBBW Edition page (PDF viewer).

  • Additional information
    The icon provides additional details such as sources, handwriting notes, etc.
