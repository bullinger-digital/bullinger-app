What you can find in the online search system

The starting point for Bullinger Digital was the information on the Bullinger correspondence provided by the Institute for Swiss Reformation Studies (IRG), the project partner and publisher of the Heinrich Bullinger Correspondence Edition. Additionally, information from other knowledge sources is available. The following overview shows the information available in the online search system:
  • Metadata for all preserved letters, compiled by the Institute for Swiss Reformation Studies (IRG).
  • Summaries (regests) for all letters published in the Heinrich Bullinger Correspondence Edition (HBBW Edition) (= letters from 1523–1547).
  • Transcriptions of letter texts
    • from the HBBW Edition
    • from other editions, e.g., Vadian Collection of Letters (ed. by Emil Arbenz 1890–1913) or Bullinger’s Correspondence with the Grisons (ed. by Traugott Schiess, 1904–1906)
    • unpublished transcriptions by the HBBW editors for volumes 16-20
    • preliminary transcriptions of yet-to-be-edited letters (= letters from 1548–1575), some with notes, summaries, or uncertainty markings
    • machine-generated transcriptions for which no transcriptions are available, but facsimiles exist
    Machine-generated transcriptions are clearly marked as such; the source of other transcriptions is indicated with .
  • Editorial notes and factual comments
    • from the HBBW Edition
    • by HBBW editors for some unpublished letters in volumes 16-20
    • as preliminary notes by HBBW editors
    The respective source is identified with .
  • Facsimiles of 11,000 letters preserved in Zurich memory institutions and 200 facsimiles from the Basel University Library, Graubünden State Archive, and Zofingen City Library. The origin of the facsimiles is displayed above the digital copies and must be correctly cited when used (see Copyright).
  • Additional facsimiles available as PDFs on www.swisscollections.ch are linked.

Which letters have facsimiles?

  • In total, facsimiles are available for 11,200 letters.
  • We distinguish between:
    • Autograph = a document written and sent by the sender themselves
    • Original = a document not written by the sender themselves but sent
    • (Autograph) Draft = a draft of the letter written by the eventual sender
    • (Autograph) Copy / Partial Copy = a copy of the letter written by the actual sender
  • If no facsimile of the autograph or original could be created, facsimiles of copies or drafts are provided where possible. These may differ from the transcribed letter text if it relates to another document type.

What editorial standards apply to Bullinger Digital?

The guiding standard is the HBBW Edition:
  • Summaries and annotations (footnotes) have been adopted 1:1 from the HBBW Edition with a few exceptions. These exceptions are:
    • Signature details for letters with facsimiles reflect the current state at the time of facsimile creation. If these differ from the signatures printed in the HBBW Edition, they are listed as "old signatures" and can be found through search.
    • The notation of footnote references has been standardized in Bullinger Digital. In the earlier volumes, footnote references followed the system a–z, aa–zz, aaa–zzz, etc. In the later volumes (and in Bullinger Digital), the notation follows the system a–z, aa–az, ba–bz, etc.
    • Marginalia printed in Volume 1 were not included.
    • Various dashes and hyphens have been standardized to a simple hyphen "-".
    • In the footnotes of edited letters, references to line numbers from the HBBW Edition are sometimes found. These line numbers have not been included in Bullinger Digital.
  • The system for automatic handwriting recognition (HTR) was trained using HBBW transcriptions and preliminary transcriptions.
  • Metadata enclosed in [ ] indicates it was inferred and not found on the letter itself. Square brackets are ignored in search queries.
  • Letters not yet published by the HBBW Edition may have metadata that differs from each other. The various versions are linked to one another.
  • Generally, metadata for letters from 1548 onwards (not yet revised by the HBBW Edition) are subject to changes and corrections.

What languages, scripts, and characters appear in Bullinger Digital?

The letters are primarily written in Latin (80%) and Early New High German (FNH German, 18%), often with language switching (code-switching) within the letters. A small number of letters (fewer than 10) are written in French, Greek, and Italian. These languages are also found in quotes and other inserts within Latin and FNH German. There are also a few Hebrew inserts.

Accordingly, the letters of the Latin alphabet, including historical special characters, primarily appear:
  • In FNH German: In addition to today's standard umlauts (äöü), umlauts with a superscript "e" (aͤ, oͤ, uͤ) and a superscript "o" (uͦ) are present, as well as the German "ß".
  • In Latin, there is also the "ę" (e-caudata).
  • In French letters (and also in footnotes), French diacritics appear (à, è, é, ì, ç, …).

Letters and quotes in Greek use the Greek alphabet, including diacritics (rough breathing, smooth breathing, acute, grave, circumflex, trema, iota subscript), and many combinations thereof.

Few Hebrew inserts use Hebrew characters.
